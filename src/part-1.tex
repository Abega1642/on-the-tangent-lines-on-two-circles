\section{First Category of Common Tangents to Two Circles}

The purpose of this section is to determine both the exact points of
tangency of the common external tangents to two circles and the
intersection point of these tangent lines.

\begin{proposition}
  Let $(C_1)$ and $(C_2)$ be two circles arranged as in the figure
  below with radius $r_1$ and $r_2$ respectively.
  Then the angle $\gamma$ formed by the tangent and the radius satisfies
  \[
    \gamma
    =
    \frac{\pi}{2}
    -
    \arcsin\!\left(\frac{r_1 - r_2}{d}\right).
  \]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
  We focus on the triangle formed by the common external tangents,
  shown in the next figure.

  \begin{center}
    \input{figures/fig-2}
  \end{center}

  \paragraph{Similar triangles (Thales' theorem).}

  From the similarity of the triangles, we obtain
  \[
    \frac{d'}{r_2} = \frac{d + d'}{r_1}.
  \]

  This yields
  \[
    r_1 d' = r_2(d + d')
    \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad
    (r_1 - r_2)d' = r_2 d,
  \]
  and therefore
  \[
    d' = \frac{r_2 d}{\,r_1 - r_2\,}.
  \]

  \paragraph{Trigonometric relation.}

  From the right triangle in Figure~2,
  \[
    \sin\!\left(\frac{\alpha}{2}\right) = \frac{r_2}{d'}.
  \]

  Substituting the expression of $d'$ gives
  \[
    \sin\!\left(\frac{\alpha}{2}\right)
    =
    \frac{r_2}{\dfrac{r_2 d}{r_1 - r_2}}
    =
    \frac{r_1 - r_2}{d}.
  \]

  Hence,
  \[
    \frac{\alpha}{2}
    =
    \arcsin\!\left(\frac{r_1 - r_2}{d}\right).
  \]

  \paragraph{Angle relation.}

  From the geometry of Figure~2,
  \[
    \pi = \frac{\pi}{2} + \frac{\alpha}{2} + \gamma,
  \]
  which implies
  \[
    \gamma = \frac{\pi}{2} - \frac{\alpha}{2}.
  \]

  \paragraph{Conclusion.}

  Substituting the value of $\dfrac{\alpha}{2}$ yields
  \[
    \boxed{
      \gamma
      =
      \frac{\pi}{2}
      -
      \arcsin\!\left(\frac{r_1 - r_2}{d}\right)
    }.
  \]

\end{proof}
